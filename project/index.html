Perspective

How Vast is Space?

The observable universe stretches about 93 billion light-years in diameter. A single light-year — the distance light travels in one year — is roughly 9.46 trillion kilometers.

Our entire solar system is a tiny speck within the Milky Way, which is itself just one of an estimated two trillion galaxies in the observable universe.
