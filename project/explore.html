Quick Facts

Did You Know?

Black Holes

A black hole's gravity is so extreme that not even light can escape once it crosses the event horizon. The nearest known black hole, Gaia BH1, is just 1,560 light-years away.

Comets

Comets are icy bodies that form a bright coma and tail as they approach the Sun. Halley's Comet, visible roughly every 75 years, has been observed since at least 240 BCE.

Nebulae

Nebulae are vast clouds of gas and dust — the birthplaces of stars. The Orion Nebula, visible to the naked eye, is one of the most studied star-forming regions in the sky.
